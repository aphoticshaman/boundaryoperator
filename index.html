About

When the uniform came off, the pattern recognition didn't.

Cardwell makes dark electronic music, paints fluid acrylics, conducts AI safety research, and writes about consciousness. He wrote a memoir called Defuzed. He's built containment architectures in Rust, disclosed vulnerabilities to Anthropic, hung paintings in Gulf Coast galleries, and put music in headphones across thirty countries.

The mathematical boundary operator — ∂ — maps objects to their edges. It's the most honest name he could have picked. Every domain he works in, he's at the edge between what's known and what isn't. Between what's safe and what detonates. Between signal and noise.

Most of this work was produced through adversarial cross-validation across five or six AI systems simultaneously — Claude, ChatGPT, Grok, Gemini, DeepSeek — a process he calls the Pod. He's one of roughly a million people the industry calls centaur users. The difference is he publishes what comes out.

Based in Pensacola, Florida.
